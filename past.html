§ 04A Century of Tampa Transit

Tampa Has Voted on Transit Before.

Three referenda have failed in the last sixteen years, each tied to broad spending the public did not trust. Here is what we learned. and what this amendment does differently.

1885. STREETCARS BEGIN
Tampa Street Railway opens horse-drawn line on Franklin St.

By 1893 the system electrifies, eventually growing to 53 miles serving Ybor, West Tampa, Sulphur Springs, and the Port. At its peak, Tampa is the densest streetcar network in Florida.

53 mi peak24M annual rides
1946. SHUT DOWN
The last Tampa streetcar runs August 4, 1946.

Following the national pattern of post-war motorization, the city dismantles its streetcar system in favor of buses and freeway construction. Rail rights-of-way are sold or paved over.

61 yr served0 mi remaining
2002. HEROIC RETURN
TECO Line Streetcar opens. 2.7 miles, Ybor to downtown.

A heritage-replica line, now fare-free, demonstrates ridership demand: 1.4M annual riders by 2024. Proves the political and physical viability of fixed-guideway in modern Tampa, but never extended.

2.7 mi1.4M rides/yr
2010. REFERENDUM I FAILED
Hillsborough 1% Transportation Tax fails 58-42.

Proposed a 1¢ surtax for a mix of rail, BRT, buses, roads, sidewalks, and bridges. Critics: too broad, no firm corridors. Supporters lacked unified turnout in unincorporated areas.

NO 58.0%YES 42.0%
2018. REFERENDUM II OVERTURNED
"All for Transportation" passes. then is voided in court.

Voters approve a 1% surtax 57-43. Florida Supreme Court strikes down spending allocations in 2021 (Robert Emerson v. Hillsborough County), citing improper restriction of BoCC discretion.

YES 57.0%VOIDED 2021
2022. REFERENDUM III FAILED
Revised 1% Transportation Surtax fails 51-49.

Narrowed allocation, removed contested provisions. Failed by ~13,000 votes. Post-election polling identified "no specific projects on the ballot" as the leading reason for No votes.

NO 51.4%YES 48.6%
The lesson
Be specific.

Voters in Hillsborough have not rejected transit. They have rejected unaccountable spending. This amendment lists corridors, locks revenue to fixed-guideway construction, and sunsets in thirty years. Specificity is the campaign.
