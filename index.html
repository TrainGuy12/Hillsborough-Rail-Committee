§ 01Overview

What this amendment does. and what it does not.

Projections show Tampa Bay's population reaching 1.68 million by 2030 and nearly 1.9 million by 2050. Without a funded transit plan, every new arrival is another car on already-overcrowded roads.

This amendment changes that. a dedicated revenue stream, locked to fixed-guideway construction, with independent oversight. Every person who boards a train is a car removed from the road. The people are coming regardless. The only question is whether we build for them

In this proposal you will find an outline of a series of potential transit systems in Hillsborough County, the projected economic impact of each phase of this system, and an achievable, direct path toward making this vision a reality.

The half-cent levy raises an estimated $128M annually at current taxable sales, of which roughly 62% comes from non-residents passing through the county. Bonded against thirty years, that revenue underwrites a federal Capital Investment Grant match sufficient to deliver 42 miles of light rail.

What it does not do: fund road expansion, fund bus operations, fund TIF districts, fund the Port, or fund any project not built within an exclusive right-of-way. Those exclusions are written into the amendment's text and are enforceable in court.
