FIXED-GUIDEWAY TRANSIT FUNDING AMENDMENT

Title. Authorizing a one-half cent (0.5%) county discretionary sales surtax dedicated solely to the construction and maintenance of fixed-guideway transit in Hillsborough County.

Summary. Shall the Charter of Hillsborough County be amended to require the Board of County Commissioners to levy a one-half-cent sales surtax, the proceeds of which shall be used exclusively for the planning, design, construction, operation, and maintenance of fixed-guideway transit options within Hillsborough County, and for connecting bus service to fixed-guideway stations, with such levy to sunset thirty (30) years following the date of first collection, and to establish an independent Citizen Oversight Board to audit expenditures annually?

Article VIII, § 9. Fixed-Guideway Transit. (a) Authorization. If this amendment is approved by the electors, the County shall, pursuant to its authority under § 212.055, Florida Statutes, issue and impose a discretionary sales surtax in the amount of one-half of one percent (0.5%) upon all transactions otherwise subject to the state sales tax and occurring within the boundaries of Hillsborough County. (b) Use of Proceeds. The proceeds of said surtax, together with all interest and investment earnings thereon, shall be deposited into a dedicated Fixed-Guideway Transit Trust Fund and shall be expended solely for the planning, design, acquisition of right-of-way, construction, equipping, operation, and maintenance of fixed-guideway transit options in Hillsborough County, including but not limited to light rail, automated guideway, and grade-separated bus rapid transit. No proceeds shall be expended for general roadway capacity or for administrative purposes unrelated to fixed-guideway transit. (c) Connecting Bus Service. Surtax proceeds may be expended, in coordination with the Hillsborough Area Regional Transit Authority, for the operation, expansion, and maintenance of feeder bus routes that directly connect to fixed-guideway stations constructed under this section, including first-mile and last-mile service, frequency improvements on routes terminating at such stations, and the alignment of bus service hours with fixed-guideway service hours. (d) Oversight. A Citizen Oversight Board of nine (9) members, appointed in the manner provided in subsection (f), shall annually retain an independent auditor to review all expenditures and shall publish such audit no later than 120 days after the close of each fiscal year. (e) Sunset. The surtax authorized herein shall expire thirty (30) years from the date of first collection unless reauthorized by the electors. (f) Implementation. The Board of County Commissioners shall, within 180 days, enact ordinances necessary to give full effect to this section.
